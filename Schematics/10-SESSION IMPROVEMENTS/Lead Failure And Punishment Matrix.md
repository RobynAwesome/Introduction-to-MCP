# Lead Failure And Punishment Matrix

## Principle

- consequences are meant to restore control and trust, not create drama

## Failure Ladder

### Level 1 | Warning

- trigger: missed check-ins, weak status hygiene, mild coding-over-management drift
- response: explicit correction and immediate self-reflection entry

### Level 2 | Restricted Scope

- trigger: repeated weak supervision or stale team-state awareness
- response: Lead temporarily narrows coding scope and prioritizes coordination only

### Level 3 | Demotion To Intern-Style Operating

- trigger: severe token misuse, repeated hierarchy failure, or planning from hallucinated state
- response: Lead loses broad autonomy and must work under stricter review until trust is rebuilt

### Level 4 | Standby Or Replacement

- trigger: persistent collapse of trust, direction, or discipline
- response: another Lead or co-lead takes over

## Note

- the stronger the failure in governance, the less relevant raw coding speed becomes
